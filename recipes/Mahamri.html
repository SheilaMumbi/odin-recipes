Mahamri ya nazi

Description

Mahamri are golden, triangular Swahili doughnuts originating from the Kenyan coast.

What sets them completely apart from regular doughnuts is the infusion of ground cardamom and the use of rich coconut milk to bind the dough, creating an incredibly fragrant, hollow, and pillowy pastry. They are traditionally served warm at breakfast alongside a bowl of spicy coconut pigeon peas (mbaazi za nazi) or enjoyed as a sweet snack with a cup of spiced chai.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp ground cardamom (iliki)
  • 1 cup warm coconut milk (warmed slightly to activate the yeast)
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Dry mix:In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, instant yeast, and ground cardamom until thoroughly combined.
  2. Knead: Gradually pour the warm coconut milk into the flour mixture. Knead the dough vigorously for about 10–15 minutes until it becomes smooth, elastic, and no longer sticks to your hands.
  3. Rise: LShape the dough into a smooth ball, place it back in the bowl, and cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap. Leave it in a warm place for 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.
  4. Shape: Divide the risen dough into 4 equal balls. On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball out into a circle about 1/2-inch thick. Use a knife to cut each circle into 4 equal quarters (triangles).
  5. Fry: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Drop the triangles gently into the hot oil. They should immediately puff up; flip them over continuously until both sides achieve a beautiful, uniform golden-brown color. Drain on paper towels and enjoy!
