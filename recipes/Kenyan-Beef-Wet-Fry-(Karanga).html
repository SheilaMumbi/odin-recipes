Kenyan Beef Wet Fry

Description

Kenyan Beef Wet Fry is a beloved national staple found in almost every household and local eatery.

It features tender, bite-sized pieces of beef fried down with heavy aromatics like red onions, garlic, ginger, and sweet bell peppers, then tossed in a thick, highly reduced tomato gravy. Unlike a traditional runny stew, a "wet fry" leaves just enough rich, concentrated sauce clinging to the meat to perfectly coat a bite of ugali or rice.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef, cubed (sirloin or chuck steak work best)
  • 2 large red onions, finely chopped
  • 3 medium tomatoes, chopped (or puréed)
  • 1 green bell pepper (hoho), sliced into strips
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste & 1 tbsp garlic paste
  • 1 tsp coriander powder & 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Fresh coriander (dania), chopped for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Boil: Place the cubed beef in a pot with a pinch of salt and a splash of water. Cover and let it simmer until the meat is tender and the water completely evaporates.
  2. Sear: Add the 2 tbsp of cooking oil directly into the same pot with the beef. Fry the meat over medium-high heat for 3–5 minutes until the edges are beautifully browned and crisp.
  3. Aromatics: Lower the heat slightly, then toss in the chopped red onions, ginger paste, and garlic paste. Stir frequently, cooking for about 4 minutes until the onions turn soft and translucent.
  4. Tomatoes & Spices: Stir in the chopped tomatoes, coriander powder, and turmeric. Cover the pot and let it cook for 5 minutes over medium-low heat until the tomatoes break down completely into a smooth paste.
  5. Finish: Add the green bell pepper slices and toss thoroughly. Cook uncovered for another 3 minutes so the pepper softens but retains a bit of crunch. Turn off the heat, stir in the fresh dania, and serve hot.
