Swahili Chicken Coconut Curry

Description

Chicken Coconut Curry is a spectacular Kenyan coastal dish where succulent chicken is first marinated in aromatic spices and grilled over an open flame to capture a deep, smoky char, then simmered in a rich, velvety coconut curry infused with fresh ginger, garlic, and green chilies.

It delivers the perfect, comforting balance of creamy sweetness and savory, smoky heat, making it an absolute standout when paired with warm, layered chapati.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken pieces (bone-in thighs or drumsticks hold up best to grilling)
  • 1 can (400ml) heavy coconut milk or coconut cream
  • Aromatics: 1 large onion, 2 medium tomatoes, 2-3 green chilies, fresh coriander (cilantro)
  • Pantry Staples: Ginger paste, garlic paste, 1 lime, cooking oil, and salt
  • Spices: Ground cumin, turmeric, coriander powder, and chili powder

Steps

  1. Marinate: Toss 1 kg chicken pieces with 1 tbsp ginger paste, 1 tbsp garlic paste, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, the juice of 1 lime, 1 tbsp oil, and salt. Let it sit for 30 minutes.
  2. Char: Grill or broil the chicken until it gets a deep, smoky char on the outside (it doesn't need to be fully cooked through yet). Set aside.
  3. Blend: Purée 1 chopped onion, 2 chopped tomatoes, and 2-3 green chilies in a blender until smooth.
  4. Cook Base: Pour the blended paste into a pot over medium heat, stir in 1 tsp coriander powder and 1/2 tsp chili powder, and simmer for 5–10 minutes until thickened.
  5. Simmer: Pour in 1 can (400ml) coconut milk, bring to a gentle simmer, then add the charred chicken. Cover and cook on low heat for 15–20 minutes until the chicken is tender and the sauce is rich and thick. Garnish with fresh coriander.
